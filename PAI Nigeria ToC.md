# **PauseAI Nigeria – Theory of Change** 

# PauseAI Nigeria’s work is grounded in the broader PauseAI Global Theory of Change, which outlines the international strategy for achieving a coordinated pause on frontier AI development.

For the full global framework, see: **\[[PauseAI Global Theory of Change](https://pauseai.info/theory-of-change)\]**

## 

## **Mission**

PauseAI Nigeria seeks to contribute to a **global halt on frontier AI development** until it can be made safe and placed under meaningful democratic control, by mobilising public awareness, policy engagement, and coordinated civic action within Nigeria.

---

## **What We Want**

A world in which **superhuman AI systems are not developed or deployed** until:

* Robust safety guarantees exist, and  
* Democratic institutions have effective oversight and control.

This pause must be **international**, not local, due to global race dynamics in AI development.

---

## **The Problem We Are Addressing (Nigeria Context)**

Despite growing global concern about AI risks:

1. **Low Policy Awareness in Nigeria**  
   * AI existential and systemic risks are largely absent from Nigerian policy conversations.  
   * Policymakers focus on short-term economic and innovation benefits, not long-term safety.  
2. **Global Race Dynamics**  
   * Countries fear being left behind economically and strategically.  
   * Even well-informed actors are incentivized to prioritize speed over safety.  
   * Nigeria risks becoming a passive rule-taker rather than an informed voice in global AI governance.  
3. **Lack of Urgency**  
   * AI progress is underestimated.  
   * The speed of recent advances has consistently surprised experts, yet this urgency has not translated into Nigerian public discourse.  
4. **Psychological Barriers & Overton Window Constraints**  
   * Existential AI risk is still seen as “too extreme” or speculative.  
   * Serious discussion of pausing AI remains taboo in political and elite circles.  
   * Public concern exists, but is under-articulated and poorly organized.

---

## **Our Core Assumption**

If **Nigerian policymakers, media, students, and civil society** understand:

* the scale of AI risks,  
* Nigeria’s stake in global AI governance, and  
* the feasibility of coordinated international action,

then Nigeria can:

* support global pause initiatives,  
* influence international negotiations,  
* and avoid being sidelined in decisions that affect humanity’s future.

---

## **How Change Happens (Pathway)**

### **Short-Term Outcomes**

* Increased public awareness of AI safety risks in Nigeria  
* Nigerian policymakers exposed to AI pause arguments  
* A visible, credible PauseAI Nigeria chapter

### **Medium-Term Outcomes**

* AI risk enters Nigerian policy and media discourse  
* Nigerian representatives become engaged in international AI governance discussions  
* Nigeria supports or aligns with international pause initiatives

### **Long-Term Impact**

* Contribution to international momentum toward:  
  * an AI pause treaty, or  
  * supply-chain–based halts on frontier AI development  
* Reduced risk of catastrophic or existential harm from uncontrolled AI

---

## **Our Core Strategies**

### **1\. Grow the Movement**

* Build a national volunteer base through:  
  * Online organizing  
  * Student engagement  
  * Transparent and accessible messaging  
* Empower volunteers to take concrete action with minimal barriers.

---

### **2\. Inform and Shape Public Discourse**

We raise awareness by:

* Publishing educational content (articles, posts, explainers)  
* Media appearances (radio, TV, podcasts, online media)  
* Public events and discussions  
* Partnerships with influencers, academics, and civil society groups

---

### **3\. Lobbying & Policy Engagement (Nigeria-Focused)**

* Engage policymakers, advisers, and regulators through:  
  * Email campaigns  
  * Meetings  
  * Policy briefs  
* Encourage Nigerian leaders to:  
  * Recognize AI existential risks  
  * Support international AI pause discussions  
  * Participate responsibly in global AI summits and treaties

---

### **4\. Protests & Visible Civic Action**

* Organize **peaceful, high-visibility actions**, adapted to Nigerian realities:  
  * **Virtual protests** to overcome logistical and security constraints  
  * Coordinated global protest moments  
* Use protests to:  
  * Shift the Overton window  
  * Generate media attention  
  * Recruit and energize volunteers

---

### **5\. Youth & University Engagement**

* Host **AI Safety debates in Nigerian universities**  
* Engage students as:  
  * Future policymakers  
  * Technologists  
  * Opinion leaders  
* Build a long-term pipeline of informed AI safety advocates.

---

## **What We Do NOT Do**

* ❌ Promote or tolerate violence  
* ❌ Take sides in unrelated political, religious, or ideological issues  
* ❌ Misrepresent facts or exaggerate claims

PauseAI Nigeria commits to **peaceful, honest, evidence-based advocacy**, aligned with PauseAI Global principles.

---

## **Nigeria-Specific Priority Actions**

Due to Nigeria’s context, PauseAI Nigeria emphasizes:

* **Lobbying and policy engagement**  
* **Strategic media outreach**  
* **Virtual protests**  
* **University debates on AI safety**

---

## **Conclusion**

PauseAI Nigeria exists to ensure that Nigeria is **not silent, unprepared, or sidelined** in decisions about humanity’s AI future. By mobilising public awareness, influencing policy discourse, and aligning with global PauseAI efforts, we aim to play a meaningful role in securing a safer future for all.
